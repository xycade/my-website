title = 'Beyond the Black Box: An Inherently Interpretable State-Aware Reinforcement Learning Framework for Trustworthy Transit Network Design'
description = "While artificial intelligence (AI) has significantly advanced urban transit planning, the pervasive use of ”black-box” deep reinforcement learning (DRL) models poses a critical challenge to regulatory auditability and stakeholder trust. To address this gap, we propose an Inherently Interpretable State-Aware Control framework, implemented as an Interpretable State-Aware Memetic Algorithm (ISA-MA). Unlike DRL approaches that obscure decision logic within high-dimensional neural architectures, the ISA-MA framework projects macroscopic evolutionary search dynamics onto a parsimonious set of semantic health indicators (convergence stagnation and population diversity). This formulation replaces opaque neural controllers with a transparent, rule-auditable tabular Q-learning agent, guaranteeing interpretability-by-design. Empirical validation across benchmark transit networks demonstrates that ISA-MA not only maintains superior solution quality and algorithmic stability but also provides a fully observable policy log. Visualizations of the learned Q-table reveal an autonomous, rational diagnosis-and-treatment strategy that mirrors expert human planning heuristics—transitioning from stability-focused refinement to targeted structural exploration. By reconciling optimization performance with mechanistic transparency, this research provides a robust, evidence-based decision-support system essential for public-sector transit governance, regulatory compliance, and long-term stakeholder confidence."
